The classtracker is a MEAN stack application designed to help teachers manage classes and lessons at an English training school in China. Teachers can add their own classes to a class list, along with the class times and comments on the class, add students to those classes along with various comments, add and edit lesson across the curriculum and add those lessons to timeslots of classes. These lessons then show up in teachers own schedule and are able to be exported to an excel file. Although there is no direct communication function, teachers are able to share classes to other teachers who will support in the teaching of those classes, and through comments on activities and other areas teachers are able to share ideas about lessons. The project can be found here, and the github repository can be found here. Family Photo Album is a Python Flask application inspired by the current COVID situation to help share photos with family and friends easily. As I am living in China and most of my family live in England this was something that I would like to be easier than sharing small groups of photos on social media (especially in China) or directly through different chatting apps. The app is built in Flask and templated with Jinja. The image files are stored on the file system of the server and accessed through the SQLite database. Users can create albums and upload photos to the albums, with some small editing abilities for the photos (mainly just the ability to rotate the images to make sure they are in the intended aspect). These albums can then be shared with other users directly by sharing to a username, or shared through the created links to both users or non-users. Individual photos can be shared to other users.
